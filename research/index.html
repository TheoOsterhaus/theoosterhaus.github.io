Research

Working papers, ongoing projects & interests

Overview

My research sits at the intersection of econometrics and machine learning. I investigate how modern ML methods (Random Forests, hyperparameter tuning, cross-validation) can complement or improve upon traditional panel regression approaches.

Research Topics

Machine Learning for Economists

Investigating classification algorithms (Decision Trees, Random Forests) and their validity compared to classic discriminant analysis. Includes ROC/AUC evaluation and hyperparameter tuning in R.

Labour & Social Mobility

Using large panel datasets (SOEP) to study intergenerational income mobility and gender wage differentials in Germany.

Panel Methods

Applying panel OLS, fixed-effects, and instrumental variable (IV) approaches to micro-econometric questions.

Collaborations

Open to collaborations with researchers in applied microeconomics, econometrics, and data science. Please reach out via contact.
