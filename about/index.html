About

Profile, research interests & background

Profile

I am a Master's student in Economics at the University of Cologne, specialising in Market Design and Behaviour.

My work lies at the intersection of econometrics, machine learning, and applied economic policy.

I am particularly interested in how data-driven methods can improve causal inference and inform policy decisions in labour markets and other economic environments.

Academic Background

I completed my B.Sc. in Economics at the University of Bonn. During my studies, I developed a strong interest in empirical methods and statistical learning.

My Bachelor's thesis, "Comparison of Discriminant Analysis and Random Forests in Classification" , evaluates classical statistical methods against modern machine learning approaches in classification tasks.

Research Interests

  • Labour economics & intergenerational mobility
  • Panel econometrics & model selection
  • Empirical industrial organisation & market design

Professional Experience
